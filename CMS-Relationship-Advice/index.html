Keystone

http://keystonejs.com/

  • Built on Express

  • Uses MongoDB

  • Lightweight, flexible & extendable

  • Large community

Keystone

  • Seemed nice enough
  • would take a bit of work to customize to the point where I would find it terribly useful
  • Moved on without much work

PencilBlue

https://pencilblue.org/

  • Business Class

  • Built to work in a clustered environment

  • Openshift compatibility

  • Does not use express

PencilBlue

  • Really liked it. Very robust
  • Was a little complicated to customize- doesn't use express
  • Got it all working. Pushed live and it didn't work right.
  • Would've been much better had I started from scratch rather than trying to fit it into my existing application

Taracot

https://taracot.org/

  • Uses MongoDB and Express

  • Redis is a requirement

  • Comes pre-packaged with example 'modules'

  • Multi-lingual and comes with Russian translations for everything

  • Basically one contributor

Taracot

  • Connecting to Redis was a little complicated
  • Easy to understand the code, make modifications
  • A little buggy in the admin console, but gets the job done
  • Not as robust as the others, but doesn't overcomplicate itself
  • Currently using in production

Picking the right CMS

  • What's the Primary Use? your results may vary
    • Blogging - Calipso
    • Narrow Focus - Keystone, Apostrophe
    • Enterprise - PencilBlue, Keystone
    • E-Commerce - TaracotJS, PencilBlue
  • Who is relying on this? Can you handle some bugs in the admin UX or will a large team be relying on everything being very smooth?
  • What's your setup?
    • Starting from scratch?
    • Existing App
    • Limited Access
    • Redis/MongoDB/Express
    • Containers

Some Parting Wisdom on Your Budding New Relationship

  • There is no 'perfect' CMS, but there might be one that's perfect for you.
  • You may need a little patience at first when you're getting to know your CMS.
  • Breaking up is hard (esp. after you've realized you've spent all your nights and weekends together), but may be the right thing.
  • You have to work a little too if you're expecting something fantastic.pro tip: that means contribute on the project
